# Report Quality Rubric

## Purpose

A high-quality engineering report enables another engineer to make informed decisions with minimal additional investigation. This rubric provides a standardized method for evaluating engineering reports, ensuring they meet Esper's standards for clarity, rigor, and professionalism.

## Characteristics of Good Reports

A good report is: evidence-driven, actionable, prioritized, balanced, transparent, technically precise, and honest about uncertainty.
Avoid reports that are: vague, repetitive, unsupported, overly speculative, excessively summarized, lacking prioritization, or missing implementation guidance.

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Completeness
- Is the scope explicitly defined?
- Are all user requirements or initial prompts addressed?
- Is the context gathered documented transparently?

### 2. Evidence Quality
- Are claims supported by concrete evidence (e.g., code snippets, logs, documentation)?
- Are observations clearly distinguished from inferences and assumptions?
- Is speculation avoided or explicitly labeled?

### 3. Prioritization
- Are findings prioritized by engineering impact rather than implementation effort?
- Is there a clear roadmap for addressing the most critical issues first?
- Are nits and minor issues separated from critical blocking concerns?

### 4. Actionability
- Do recommendations provide clear, next-step guidance?
- Can another engineer begin implementation with minimal clarification?
- Are the technical consequences of the findings explained?

### 5. Clarity
- Is the language concise and technically precise?
- Is the report structured logically, making it easy to scan?
- Does it avoid generic statements and repetitive phrasing?

### 6. Confidence Communication
- Is uncertainty stated honestly using the confidence taxonomy (Confirmed, Likely, Speculative)?
- Is the overall confidence in the assessment communicated clearly?

### 7. Balanced Assessment
- Are architectural and engineering strengths identified and documented?
- Does the report recognize good decisions worth preserving?

## Usage

Use this rubric when reviewing, editing, or producing any engineering deliverable to ensure it reflects the communication standards of a senior engineer. Deliverables should be validated using `checklists/self-review.md`.
